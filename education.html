Cut

The cut determines how well a diamond reflects light, creating its brilliance and sparkle. It's the most important factor affecting a diamond's beauty.

Cut Grades:

Excellent Maximum brilliance
Very Good High brilliance
Good Good brilliance
Fair Some brilliance

Clarity

Clarity refers to the absence of inclusions and blemishes. The clearer the diamond, the more rare and valuable it becomes.

Clarity Grades:

FL Flawless
IF Internally Flawless
VVS1-VVS2 Very Very Slightly Included
VS1-VS2 Very Slightly Included
SI1-SI2 Slightly Included

Color

Diamond color is graded from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow). The closer to colorless, the more valuable the diamond.

Color Grades:

D-F Colorless
G-J Near Colorless
K-M Faint Yellow
N-R Very Light Yellow
S-Z Light Yellow

Jewelry Care Guide

Daily Care

  • Remove jewelry when washing hands or using lotions
  • Store pieces separately to prevent scratching
  • Avoid contact with harsh chemicals
  • Put jewelry on last when getting dressed
  • Remove first when undressing
  • Check prongs and settings regularly

‚ú®
Cleaning Tips

  • Use mild soap and warm water for regular cleaning
  • Gently brush with a soft toothbrush
  • Dry with a lint-free cloth
  • Professional cleaning every 6 months
  • Avoid ultrasonic cleaners for delicate stones
  • Never use bleach or harsh chemicals

üè†
Storage Tips

  • Store in individual soft pouches or compartments
  • Keep away from direct sunlight
  • Use anti-tarnish strips for silver pieces
  • Avoid extreme temperatures
  • Consider a jewelry box with fabric lining
  • Insurance appraisal every 2-3 years

Diamond Certifications

GIA

Gemological Institute of America

The world's most trusted source for diamond grading and gem identification. GIA created the 4Cs and the International Diamond Grading System.

IGI

International Gemological Institute

One of the largest independent jewelry certification organizations, known for consistent and reliable diamond grading standards.

HRD

HRD Antwerp

European leader in diamond certification, backed by decades of expertise from the diamond capital of the world, Antwerp.

SSEF

Swiss Gemmological Institute

Renowned for colored gemstone identification and advanced research in gemstone treatments and synthetic detection.
